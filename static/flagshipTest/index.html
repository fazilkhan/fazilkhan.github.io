At most state flagship universities – the jewels in the crown of state public universities – Black and Latino students are still very much under-represented.

In 14 states, the gap between the percentage of public high school graduates who are Black and the percentage of students who enroll in the state flagship are Black was 10 points or more in 2021.

Some of the worst performing states are located in the South.

In Mississippi, 41 percent of high school graduates were Black but only 8 percent of incoming freshmen at Ole Miss, the state’s flagship, were Black.

The gap at the University of Georgia has grown over the past three years and now stands at 31 percentage points. The university doesn’t consider race in admissions.
